The Oud House Guide

New to oil-based perfume? A little goes a long way. Here's how to get the most from every drop.

How to apply

Dab — never rub. Touch the applicator or a drop to your pulse points: wrists, the base of the throat, behind the ears. The warmth of your skin releases the scent slowly through the day. Pressing your wrists together bruises the top notes, so let it settle on its own.

Why oil perfumes last longer

Oil-based perfumes carry no alcohol, so nothing flashes off in the first minutes. The fragrance sits on the skin and unfolds with your body heat — richer, closer to the skin, and far longer-lasting than a typical spray. A single application often carries from morning into evening.

Layering

Oils are made for layering. Wear a warm amber or oud as your base and lift it with something fresh or floral on top to make a scent that is entirely yours. Start with one drop of each and build slowly.

Care & storage

Keep your oils somewhere cool and dark — away from windows, radiators and the bathroom shelf. Sealed and out of sunlight, a good oil perfume keeps its character for years.
